Abstract

Healthcare delivery and payment systems in Kenya face persistent challenges, including centralized data storage, frequent data breaches, opaque payment and insurance processes, and limited patient control over medical information. Existing platforms, while providing mobile-based health services, remain vulnerable to security risks and inefficiencies that compromise patient privacy and operational transparency. This paper proposes Axiom, a decentralized healthcare payment and data management platform built on the Cardano blockchain. Axiom leverages smart contracts, on-chain verification, and off-chain encrypted data storage to create a secure, transparent, and privacy-conscious healthcare ecosystem. The platform aims to improve patient trust, streamline insurance claim processing, and enhance auditability, while ensuring compliance with privacy regulations.

1. Introduction

Healthcare systems in Kenya are increasingly adopting digital solutions for payments and recordkeeping. Platforms such as mTiba have demonstrated the benefits of mobile health wallets, but their centralized architecture exposes sensitive patient information to breaches and limits transparency in transactions and claims. Axiom addresses these gaps by introducing a blockchain-based solution that decentralizes financial and medical data processes. Using Cardanoâ€™s proof-of-stake network, Plutus smart contracts, and secure off-chain storage, Axiom allows patients, clinics, and insurers to interact seamlessly, with verifiable and auditable transactions. Sensitive data remains encrypted off-chain, while cryptographic hashes are stored on-chain to ensure data integrity without compromising privacy. By decentralizing healthcare payments and data verification, Axiom provides a trustless ecosystem that is scalable, secure, and aligned with Kenyan healthcare needs, supporting patients, clinics, and insurers in a transparent and efficient manner.
